The skill gap nobody talks about in AI engineering: 📐

It's not prompt engineering. It's not fine-tuning. It's not RAG.

It's system design.

Most AI demos fail in production because engineers focus on the AI part
and ignore the boring infrastructure: error handling, rate limiting,
audit logging, rollback, human oversight.

As a Senior Backend Engineer moving into agentic AI, I've found
that classic SE skills are more valuable than ever.

What skill do you think is most underrated in the AI space?
